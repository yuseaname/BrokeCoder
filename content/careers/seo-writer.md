# Content Writer (SEO Writer)

Hook: Help people find great answers—and help businesses win organic traffic.

## What You Do
You write useful, search-friendly content. You identify search intent, plan outlines, write drafts, and optimize for readability and on-page SEO.

### Daily Work
- Keyword research and competitive analysis
- Outlining articles and briefs
- Writing drafts and revisions
- Optimizing titles, H2/H3, FAQs, internal links
- Publishing and basic CMS management
- Reporting: rankings, traffic, and updates

### Deliverables
- Keyword map and content plan
- SEO briefs and outlines
- 1,500–3,000 word articles
- On-page SEO optimization checklist
- Internal link recommendations

## Who This Is Good For
- Clear communicators who like structure
- Curious learners who enjoy research
- Writers who enjoy practical, helpful content

## Skills You Need
### Hard Skills
- Keyword research (Ahrefs, Semrush, manual SERP)
- On-page SEO and internal linking
- Content structure: headings, scannability, FAQs
- Basic HTML/CMS familiarity

### Soft Skills
- Project management and deadlines
- Clear communication with editors/clients
- Iteration: feedback and updates

## Tools You’ll Use
- Research: Ahrefs, Semrush, Google Search Console
- Writing: Google Docs, Notion, Grammarly
- CMS: WordPress, Webflow, Ghost

## Learning Roadmap (0–90 Days)
### 0–30 Days (Foundation)
- Study 30 ranking articles in one niche
- Build keyword map: TOFU/MOFU/BOFU categories
- Write 5 articles (1,500–2,000 words) with on-page checklists

### 30–60 Days (Portfolio + Systems)
- Create content briefs with intent, headings, and FAQs
- Publish on a test blog; track rankings in a spreadsheet
- Add internal links and update older posts

### 60–90 Days (Client-Ready)
- Pitch 30 prospects with 3 topic ideas each
- Offer pilot article + keyword research bundle
- Deliver case study with ranking gains

## Portfolio Projects (3–6)
- Topic cluster (pillar + 3–5 supporting posts)
- “From zero to ranking” case study (3 months)
- Internal linking map with outcomes
- Content brief template + examples

## How to Get Your First Client/Job
1. Pick a niche and draft 5 strong samples
2. Build a simple site to host samples and briefs
3. Pitch helpful topic ideas to editors/brands
4. Offer a pilot article with guaranteed revisions
5. Deliver fast + ask for testimonials

## Pricing & Salary Ranges (No Guarantees)
- Employment: $45k–$75k entry; $65k–$95k mid-level
- Freelance: $0.08–$0.25/word; $200–$600 per article starter
- Packages: $900–$2,500 topic clusters (briefs + drafts)

> Disclaimer: Income depends on niche, deliverable quality, volume, and proof of rankings. No guarantees.

## Common Mistakes & Fixes
- Keyword stuffing → Write for humans, optimize lightly
- Thin content → Add examples, steps, visuals, FAQs
- Skipping internal links → Build clusters and cross-link
- Ignoring updates → Refresh outdated posts quarterly

## FAQs
- Do I need expensive tools? You can start manual and upgrade later.
- How long till rankings? 3–6 months is common; depends on site authority.
- Do I need a niche? It helps you pitch strong ideas and write faster.
- How many words per post? Write to cover intent; most 1.5k–3k.
