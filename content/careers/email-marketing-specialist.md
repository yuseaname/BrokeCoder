# Email Marketing Specialist

Hook: Turn inbox attention into relationships—and reliable revenue.

## What You Do
You plan email strategy, write sequences, set automations, and report results.

### Daily Work
- Write newsletters and promotional emails
- Build automations (welcome, onboarding, win-back)
- Segment lists and personalize messaging
- Track open rate, click rate, revenue per send

### Deliverables
- Email calendar and segmentation plan
- Sequences (welcome, onboarding, launch)
- Automation maps and documentation
- KPI reports and next-step tests

## Who This Is Good For
- Writers who enjoy consistency and relationship-building
- Marketers who like systems and measurement

## Skills You Need
### Hard Skills
- Copywriting and offer stacking
- List hygiene and deliverability basics
- Segmentation, tagging, personalization
- Basic HTML and template editing

### Soft Skills
- Process discipline and cadence
- Collaboration with product, sales, support
- Generosity-first, value-driven approach

## Tools You’ll Use
- Platforms: Klaviyo, ConvertKit, Mailchimp, HubSpot
- Analytics: GA4, revenue attribution
- Docs/Notion for SOPs and calendars

## Learning Roadmap (0–90 Days)
### 0–30 Days (Foundation)
- Study 20 top newsletters in one niche
- Write a 5-email welcome sequence
- Build a monthly email calendar

### 30–60 Days (Portfolio + Systems)
- Launch sequences for a test brand; track metrics
- Create email design templates and snippets
- Publish 2 case studies with outcomes

### 60–90 Days (Client-Ready)
- Pitch 20 prospects with quick-win ideas
- Offer pilot (audit + welcome + newsletter)
- Gather testimonials and permissions

## Portfolio Projects (3–6)
- Welcome sequence + KPI improvements
- Launch sequence with revenue documented
- Newsletter format optimization (A/B tests)
- Segmentation plan and automations map

## How to Get Your First Client/Job
1. Build sequences for a test brand
2. Publish metrics simply and honestly
3. Pitch helpful ideas; offer pilot package
4. Deliver fast and ask for testimonials

## Pricing & Salary Ranges (No Guarantees)
- Employment: $50k–$85k entry; $70k–$110k mid-level
- Freelance: $400–$2,000 sequences; $500–$2,500/mo retainers

> Disclaimer: Revenue per send varies widely based on list size, product-market fit, and deliverability.

## Common Mistakes & Fixes
- Over-sending promos → Balance value with sales; keep goodwill
- Poor list hygiene → Remove inactive; warm up domain; DMARC/SPF
- No testing → Experiment with subject lines, content, cadence

## FAQs
- Are emails still effective? Yes—if useful, consistent, and respectful.
- How often to send? Weekly is common; cadence depends on audience.
- Do I need coding? Minimal; templates and snippets suffice.
